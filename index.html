What is a Network? Definition of a Network:

A network is a collection of computers, devices, and other equipment connected together for the purpose of sharing resources,
such as data, files, and internet access. Networks can be used for communication, accessing shared devices like printers, or sharing data between computers.

Resource Sharing: Networks allow multiple devices to share resources such as printers, files, and internet connections. Communication: Networks enable communication between devices via email, instant messaging, and other methods. Data Sharing and Storage: With networks, large amounts of data can be shared and stored on central servers, ensuring easy access from various devices. Collaboration: Networks provide the framework for real-time collaboration, such as video conferencing and document sharing.

Types of Networks LAN (Local Area Network):

A LAN is a network confined to a small geographical area, such as a single building or a campus. It connects devices like computers, printers, and servers to share resources. Examples: Home networks, office networks.

WAN (Wide Area Network):

A WAN is a network that spans a large geographical area,A WAN is a network that spans a large geographical area, connecting multiple LANs across cities, countries, or even continents. Examples: The internet, corporate networks connecting branches across the globe.

Metropolitan Area Network:

A MAN is a network that covers a larger geographic area than a LAN but is smaller than a WAN, typically spanning a city or large campus. Examples: A network connecting all the buildings of a university or a local government network.

WLAN Wireless Local Area Network:

WLAN is a wireless version of a LAN, where devices connect over radio waves instead of physical cables. Examples: Wi-Fi networks in homes, cafes, and offices.
